## TL;DR

The **Hermes Idea Factory** is a self-driving Kanban board powered by Hermes agent. You drop in one idea. A team of AI agents plans it, builds it, and ships it for you. Idea in, finished thing out — in minutes.

---

## The Core Idea

> Drop one idea in. Approve the plan once. Let the board do the rest.

### Old Way vs New Way

| Old Way | New Way |
|---------|---------|
| You drag cards, write tickets, do the work yourself | You drop an idea in |
| You feel guilty when you don't finish | Agents read it, classify it, write a plan |
| Everything stops when you stop | A project manager + sub agents build in the background |
| You babysit every step | You only step in once to approve the plan |

---

## The 5 Floors (The Framework)

Think of it like an elevator — your idea rides from top to bottom.

| Floor | What Happens |
|-------|-------------|
| **1. Capture** | Type your idea and hit enter. That's it. |
| **2. Classify** | Agents shape the idea and build a plan around it |
| **3. The Gate** | Agents show you the plan. You approve or reject. Your only human-in-the-loop moment. |
| **4. Implement** | A project manager and sub agents build the thing in the background |
| **5. Gallery** | Everything you build shows up here. Preview live, full screen, anytime |

---

## How to Use It

1. **Drop your idea** into the capture section. Keep it simple: *"create a new SEO blog for my coffee brand"*
2. **Let the agents shape it** — they classify the idea and build a plan
3. **Check the plan** in the human approval section — it tells you what it is, how it will approach it, first milestones, and which agents will build it
4. **Hit approve and build** — go do something else, it keeps working in the background
5. **Watch it move across the board** so you always know where things are at
6. **Open the gallery** when done — preview it live, go full screen

---

## The Obsidian Memory Vault

The whole system lives inside your Obsidian vault — a second brain.

- Everything you build gets logged in one place
- Agents remember what you made before and keep building on it
- Come back days later and say *"that blog you made, improve the UI"* — it already knows what the project is, where it's stored, and how it was built
- No re-explaining

---

## The Quality Self-Checker

- Reviews its own work before handing it back
- Makes sure output is actually good
- Cleaner results without babysitting

## Overview

The **Hermes Idea Factory** is a self-driving Kanban board powered by Hermes agent. You drop in one idea. A team of AI agents plans it, builds it, and ships it for you. All in one place.

> "Idea in. Finished thing out. In minutes. That can be a website, a tool, a game, or an app. It builds while you go do something else."

---

## Why It Beats the Old Way

### Old Way
- You drag cards around a to-do list
- You write tickets
- You do most of the work yourself
- You feel guilty when you don't finish

### New Way
- You drop an idea in
- The agents read it and decide what it is
- They write a plan
- A project manager and sub agents build it
- It ships into a gallery you can preview live
- The board does the work — you stay in control

---

## The 5 Floors (The Framework)

Think of it like an elevator. Your idea rides from the top floor to the bottom. You only step in once.

### Floor 1: Capture
Type your idea and hit enter. That's the whole step.

**Example ideas:**
- "Create a new SEO blog for my coffee brand"
- "Build a landing page for my coaching business"
- "Make a tool that converts PDFs to markdown"

### Floor 2: Classify
The agents shape your idea and build a plan around it. They:
- Determine what type of project it is
- Define the approach
- Set milestones
- Assign agents to build it

### Floor 3: The Gate
The agents show you the plan. This is your human-in-the-loop moment.

The plan tells you:
- What it is
- How it will approach it
- The first milestones
- Which agents will build it

**You approve it or reject it.** This is the only time you need to step in.

### Floor 4: Implement
A project manager and sub agents build the thing for you in the background.

- Works while you do something else
- Moves across the board so you always know where things are at
- Self-checks quality before finishing

### Floor 5: Gallery
Everything you build shows up here.

- Preview it live
- Go full screen
- Come back to it whenever you want

## How to Use It — Step by Step

| Step | Action | What Happens |
|------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | Drop your idea into the capture section | Keep it simple — plain English |
| 2 | Click "let the agents shape it" | Agents classify the idea and build a plan |
| 3 | Check the plan in the human approval section | Review what, how, milestones, agents |
| 4 | Hit "approve and build" | Go do something else — it works in background |
| 5 | Watch it move across the board | Always know where things are at |
| 6 | Open the gallery when done | Preview live, go full screen |

## The Obsidian Memory Vault

The whole thing lives inside your Obsidian vault. Think of it as a second brain.

**Key benefits:**
- Everything you build gets logged in one place
- Your agents remember what you made before
- Agents keep building on previous work
- Come back days later and say *"that blog you made, improve the UI"*
- It already knows what the project is, where it's stored, and how it was built
- **No re-explaining**

> "Your agents remember what you made before and keep building on it."

---

## The Quality Self-Checker

Sometimes the first outputs aren't great. The self-checker:
- Reviews its own work
- Makes sure it's actually good before handing it back
- Delivers cleaner results without babysitting

## Which Model to Use

- Builds better quality stuff
- You're not going to run out of tokens

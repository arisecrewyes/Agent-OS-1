## Key Concepts

### Why AI Content Gets Detected

- **Perplexity** — AI uses predictable word choices; humans are more surprising
- **Burstiness** — humans vary sentence length; AI tends toward uniformity
- **Statistical patterns** — AI probability distributions leave detectable fingerprints
- **Lack of personal voice** — AI lacks idiosyncratic style, opinion, lived experience

### The Humanization Framework

1. **Generate** — AI first draft (Claude, ChatGPT, etc.)
2. **Restructure** — Reorder, combine, split paragraphs
3. **Rewrite** — Replace predictable word choices
4. **Inject** — Add personal anecdotes, opinions, examples, imperfections
5. **Vary** — Adjust sentence length, rhythm, complexity (burstiness)
6. **Verify** — Test against AI detectors, iterate until clean

### Anti-AI Writing Characteristics

- Varied sentence length (short + long mix)
- Controlled imperfection (colloquialisms, contractions, informal phrasing)
- Personal perspective (first-person, opinions, experiences)
- Domain-specific language (jargon AI over-explains)
- Emotional texture (frustration, excitement, humor)
- Structural unpredictability (no templates)

## Practical Techniques

### Sentence Restructuring

**AI-like:**
> AI tools have revolutionized content creation. They allow writers to produce articles at scale. However, AI detectors can identify machine-generated text.

**Humanized:**
> Here's the thing about AI content tools: they're incredible — until your content gets flagged. I've had entire batches of articles marked as "100% AI-generated" even after spending an hour editing them.

### Voice Injection Markers

- "I" statements and personal experience
- Specific tools, dates, and results
- Opinions not everyone agrees with
- Conversational transitions ("Look,", "Here's the thing", "Real talk:")

### Perplexity Boosting Word Swaps

| AI Default | Human Alternative |
|---|---|
| utilize | use |
| however | but / though / that said |
| it is important to note | worth noting: / heads up: |
| in conclusion | so what does this mean? |
| furthermore | and here's the kicker: |
| significant | huge / massive / game-changing |

> **Best approach:** Manual editing > automated tools. Human judgment is most reliable.

## Step-by-Step Workflow

1. **Generate** base content with Claude/ChatGPT
2. **Detect** — run through AI detector, note flagged sections
3. **Humanize** — apply framework (restructure, rewrite, inject, vary)
4. **Authenticate** — add anecdotes, opinions, specifics
5. **Verify** — re-check with 2-3 detectors:
   - GPTZero: < 20% AI probability
   - Originality.ai: > 80% originality
   - Copyleaks: < 30% AI similarity
6. **Polish** — read aloud, iterate if needed

## Common Mistakes

1. Over-relying on humanization tools (awkward output)
2. Removing ALL AI markers (some patterns = good writing)
3. Ignoring context (technical vs. casual need different approaches)
4. One-pass editing (plan for 2-3 rounds)
5. Forgetting to add value (humanized AI still needs to be useful)

# 19th June: Loop Engineering

> The system that quality controls your AI outputs automatically so you stop being the bottleneck.

## The Problem

How many times have you asked AI for something and the output just wasn't good enough?

1. You read it
2. You spotted what was wrong
3. You typed out the feedback
4. You pasted it back in
5. You did this five times before finally getting something okay

By the end: tired, bored, settled for mediocre because you ran out of energy.

**You were the loop.**

---

## What Is The Hermes Loop Engine?

A simple system with one job: **quality control your AI outputs for you.**

> You set the bar once, you walk away, and you come back to a finished result.

### How It Works

1. You tell it what "done" means
2. The builder writes a draft
3. The judge grades it out of 100
4. If not good enough → loops back and fixes itself
5. Keeps looping until it passes

**Best part:** You can use free or cheap models to run the whole thing.

---

## Wrong Beliefs vs Right Beliefs

| Wrong Belief | Right Belief |
|--------------|--------------|
| "I should just learn to prompt better and do it all myself" | A better prompt still puts you in the chair for every round. The win is not being in the loop at all. |
| "AI grading AI is a gimmick — it will rubber stamp itself" | The model that writes never grades its own work. A separate adversarial panel grades it and is told to find holes. That gap is why the final result is good. |
| "I'll set this up later when I have time" | Later is only later because you don't have time right now. That's exactly why you should set it up. |

## The Five Part Framework

### Part 1: Define Done

Write down what a great result actually looks like. Be specific.

> *"A five line cold email for agency owners that has one CTA and reads like a human wrote it."*

The more honest you are with the bar, the better the judge can grade it.

### Part 2: The Builder Acts

- Give it a starting point (optional)
- Pick your builder model:
  - **MiniMax** — good default
  - **N2** — free model on news portal
  - **OpenRouter** — free API
  - **Opus 4.8** — frontier model
- It writes the first version

### Part 3: The Judge Grades It

- A separate model grades the draft out of 100
- Lists exactly what's wrong
- Told to be adversarial and find the holes
- Example: GLM 5.2 might approve a round if it passes

**The key:** The builder never grades its own work. A separate judge does.

### Part 4: The Loop Runs

- If the draft fails → notes go back to the builder
- Builder fixes them — round after round
- Real example:
  - Round 1: 54
  - Round 2: 71
  - Round 3: 83
  - Round 4: 92
- You set max rounds so it doesn't go forever

### Part 5: Come Back To A Finished Result

- When the judge passes it → you get the final result
- Full record of every round (scores, issues fixed)
- Everything saved to your agent OS in the loop section
- You didn't have to babysit a thing

## Common Objections (And Why They're Wrong)

- **"My work is too nuanced for a machine to judge"** → The machine doesn't decide what's good. You do. You write the bar. The judge just checks against your standard.
- **"Loops sound expensive"** → Run free models with Hermes. Free APIs on OpenRouter and free models on news portal.
- **"This sounds like coding"** → No. It's a text box. Type a sentence about what good looks like and press a button.
